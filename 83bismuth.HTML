BISMUTH

Bismuth is a chemical element with the symbol Bi and atomic number 83. It is a pentavalent post-transition metal and one of the pnictogens with chemical properties resembling its lighter group 15 siblings arsenic and antimony. Elemental bismuth may occur naturally, although its sulfide and oxide form important commercial ores. The free element is 86% as dense as lead. It is a brittle metal with a silvery white color when freshly produced, but surface oxidation can give it an iridescent tinge in numerous colours. Bismuth is the most naturally diamagnetic element, and has one of the lowest values of thermal conductivity among metals.More...

PHYSICAL PROPERITIES

  • Phase at STP - solid
  • Melting point - 544.7 K ​(271.5 °C, ​520.7 °F)
  • Boiling point - 1837 K ​(1564 °C, ​2847 °F)
  • Density (at liquid) - 10.05 g/cm3
  • Heat of fusion - 11.30 kJ/mol
  • Heat of vaporization - 175 kJ/mol
  • Molar heat capacity - 25.52 J/(mol·K)

OTHER PROPERITIES

  • Crystal structure - ​rhombohedral
  • Thermal expansion - 13.4 µm/(m·K) (at 25 °C)
  • Thermal conductivity - 7.97 W/(m·K)
  • Electrical resistivity - 1.29 nΩ·m (at 20 °C)
  • Young's modulus - 32 GPa
  • Bulk modulus - 31 GPa
  • CAS Number - 7440-69-9

USES OF BISMUTH

  • Some manufacturers use bismuth as a substitute in equipment for potable water systems such as valves to meet "lead-free" mandates in the U.S.
  • Bismuth compounds (including sodium bismuth tartrate) were formerly used to treat syphilis
  • "Milk of bismuth" (an aqueous suspension of bismuth hydroxide and bismuth subcarbonate) was marketed as an alimentary cure-all in the early 20th century.
