GADOLINIUM

Gadolinium is a chemical element with the symbol Gd and atomic number 64. Gadolinium is a silvery-white metal when oxidation is removed. It is only slightly malleable and is a ductile rare-earth element. Gadolinium reacts with atmospheric oxygen or moisture slowly to form a black coating. Gadolinium below its Curie point of 20 °C (68 °F) is ferromagnetic, with an attraction to a magnetic field higher than that of nickel. GENERAL USES OF GADOLINIUM

  • Gadolinium has useful properties in alloys.
  • Gadolinium is excellent at absorbing neutrons, and so is used in the core of nuclear reactors.
  • Its compounds are useful in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), particularly in diagnosing cancerous tumours.
  • It is also used in alloys for making magnets, electronic components and data storage disks.
