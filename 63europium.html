EUROPIUM

Europium is a chemical element with the symbol Eu and atomic number 63. Europium is the most reactive lanthanide by far, having to be stored under an inert fluid to protect it from atmospheric oxygen or moisture. Europium is also the softest lanthanide, as it can be dented with a fingernail and easily cut with a knife. When oxidation is removed a shiny-white metal is visible. Europium was isolated in 1901 and is named after the continent of Europe.More...

GENERAL USES OF EUROPIUM

  • Europium is used in the printing of euro banknotes.
  • Europium is excellent at absorbing neutrons, making it valuable in control rods for nuclear reactors.
  • Europium-doped plastic has been used as a laser material. It is also used in making thin super-conducting alloys.
  • Low-energy light bulbs contain a little europium to give a more natural light.
