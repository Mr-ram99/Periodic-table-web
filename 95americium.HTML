AMERICIUM

Americium is a synthetic radioactive chemical element with the symbol Am and atomic number 95. It is a transuranic member of the actinide series, in the periodic table located under the lanthanide element europium, and thus by analogy was named after the Americas.More...

PHYSICAL PROPERITIES

  • Phase at STP - solid
  • Melting point - 1449 K ​(1176 °C, ​2149 °F)
  • Boiling point - 2880 K ​(2607 °C, ​4725 °F)
  • Density - 12 g/cm3
  • Heat of fusion - 14.39 kJ/mol
  • Molar heat capacity - 28 J/(mol·K)

OTHER PROPERITIES

  • Crystal structure - ​double hexagonal close-packed (dhcp)
  • Magnetic ordering - paramagnetic
  • Thermal conductivity - 10 W/(m·K)
  • Electrical resistivity - 0.69 nΩ·m (at 20 °C)
  • CAS Number - 7440-35-9

USES OF AMERICIUM

  • Americium is used in the most common type of household smoke detector, which uses 241Am in the form of americium dioxide as its source of ionizing radiation.
  • Americium is a starting material for the production of other transuranic elements and transactinides.
