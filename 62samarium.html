SAMARIUM

Samarium is a chemical element with the symbol Sm and atomic number 62. It is a moderately hard silvery metal that slowly oxidizes in air. Being a typical member of the lanthanide series, samarium usually assumes the oxidation state +3. Compounds of samarium(II) are also known, most notably the monoxide SmO, monochalcogenides SmS, SmSe and SmTe, as well as samarium(II) iodide. The last compound is a common reducing agent in chemical synthesis. Samarium has no significant biological role but is only slightly toxic.More...

GENERAL USES OF SAMARIUM

  • Samarium-cobalt magnets are much more powerful than iron magnets,so they are used in microwave applications.
  • Samarium is used to dope calcium chloride crystals for use in optical lasers.
  • It is also used in infrared absorbing glass and as a neutron absorber in nuclear reactors.
  • Samarium oxide finds specialised use in glass and ceramics.
  • samarium is used in carbon arc lighting for studio lighting and projection
