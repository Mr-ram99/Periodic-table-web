TANTALUM

Tantalum is a chemical element with the symbol Ta and atomic number 73. Previously known as tantalium, it is named after Tantalus, a villain from Greek mythology.Tantalum is a rare, hard, blue-gray, lustrous transition metal that is highly corrosion-resistant. It is part of the refractory metals group, which are widely used as minor components in alloys. The chemical inertness of tantalum makes it a valuable substance for laboratory equipment, and as a substitute for platinum. Its main use today is in tantalum capacitors in electronic equipment such as mobile phones, DVD players, video game systems and computers.More...

GENERAL USES OF TANTALUM

  • One of the main uses of tantalum is in the production of electronic components.
  • Tantalum is widely used in making surgical instruments and implants.
  • Tantalum was extensively used in the production of ultra high frequency electron tubes for radio transmitters.
