TUNGSTEN

Tungsten, or wolfram,is a chemical element with the symbol W and atomic number 74. Tungsten is a rare metal found naturally on Earth almost exclusively combined with other elements in chemical compounds. It was identified as a new element in 1781 and first isolated as a metal in 1783. Its important ores include scheelite, and wolframite, lending the element its alternate name.More...

GENERAL USES OF TUNGSTEN

  • Tungsten was used extensively for the filaments of old-style incandescent light bulbs.
  • Tungsten alloys are used in arc-welding electrodes and many high temeperature applications.
  • Tungsten carbides are used in makin drilling and cutting tools.
  • It is also used in manufacturing of many hard metals.
