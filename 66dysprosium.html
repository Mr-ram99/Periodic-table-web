DYSPROSIUM

Dysprosium is a chemical element with the symbol Dy and atomic number 66. It is a rare-earth element with a metallic silver luster. Dysprosium is never found in nature as a free element, though it is found in various minerals, such as xenotime. Naturally occurring dysprosium is composed of seven isotopes, the most abundant of which is 164Dy.More...

GENERAL USES OF DYSPROSIUM

  • Dysprosium’s main use is in alloys for neodymium-based magnets.
  • It is resistant to demagnetisation so,it is used in generators and magnets.
  • Dysprosium iodide is used in halide discharge lamps
  • A dysprosium oxide-nickel cermet (a composite material of ceramic and metal) is used in nuclear reactor control rods.
