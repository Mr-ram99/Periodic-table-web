GERMANIUM

Germanium is a chemical element with the symbol Ge and atomic number 32. It is a lustrous, hard-brittle, grayish-white metalloid in the carbon group, chemically similar to its group neighbors silicon and tin. Pure germanium is a semiconductor with an appearance similar to elemental silicon. Like silicon, germanium naturally reacts and forms complexes with oxygen in nature. Because it seldom appears in high concentration, germanium was discovered comparatively late in the history of chemistry. Germanium ranks near fiftieth in relative abundance of the elements in the Earth's crust. In 1869, Dmitri Mendeleev predicted its existence and some of its properties from its position on his periodic table, and called the element ekasilicon.More...

PHYSICAL PROPERITIES

  • Phase at STP - solid
  • Melting point - 1211.40 K ​(938.25 °C, ​1720.85 °F)
  • Boiling point - 3106 K ​(2833 °C, ​5131 °F)
  • Density (at liquid) - 5.60 g/cm3
  • Heat of fusion - 36.94 kJ/mol
  • Heat of vaporization - 334 kJ/mol
  • Molar heat capacity - 23.222 J/(mol·K)

OTHER PROPERITIES

  • Crystal structure - ​face-centered diamond-cubic
  • Thermal expansion - 6.0 µm/(m·K)
  • Thermal conductivity - 60.2 W/(m·K)
  • Electrical resistivity - 1 Ω·m (at 20 °C)
  • Young's modulus - 103 GPa
  • Bulk modulus - 75 GPa
  • CAS Number - 7440-56-4

GENERAL USES OF GERMANIUM

  • The major end uses for germanium in 2007, worldwide, were estimated to be: 35% for fiber-optics, 30% infrared optics, 15% polymerization catalysts, and 15% electronics and solar electric applications.
  • Silicon-germanium alloys are rapidly becoming an important semiconductor material for high-speed integrated circuits.
  • Solar panels are a major use of germanium.
